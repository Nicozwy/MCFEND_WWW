Audio samples of "Learning noise-independent speech representations for high-quality voice conversion for noisy target speakers"

Authors: Liumeng Xue, Shan Yang, Na Hu, Dan Su, Lei Xie

Abstract: Voice conversion is a technique that transforms the speech signal of a source speaker to a sound like that of a target speaker without changing linguistic content. Based on the clean data, Voice conversion has made great progress in terms of speech quality and speaker similarity. However, many acoustic environments are noisy and reverberant in real applications, severely degrading the performance and restricting the applicability of the VC methods. To address this limitation, we introduce FlowVC, a new high-quality VC framework for noisy target speakers. Our model is composed of a noise-controllable WaveGAN and a flow-based conversion model. The noise-controllable WaveGAN which is a GAN-base VAE with FiLM is proposed to learn noise-independent latent speech representations from waveform via the encoder, and reconstruct the clean waveform via the decoder and the FiLM layer. The flow-based conversion model is used to directly model the distribution of the speech representations from phoneme posteriorgram (PPG). Objective and subjective experimental results demonstrate that FlowVC achieves high speech quality and speaker similarity for the noisy target speakers.
